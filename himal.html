About Nepal

A land of wonder

Nepal is a small, landlocked country nestled between China and India, home to some of the world's most dramatic landscapes. From the tropical lowlands of the Terai to the icy peaks of the Himalayas, its geography is staggering in its variety.

Kathmandu, the capital, is a vibrant mix of ancient shrines, bustling bazaars, and modern life. The city's Durbar Squares and living goddess tradition offer a window into a culture that has flourished for over 2,000 years.

Nepal is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama — the Buddha — and a spiritual crossroads for Hinduism and Buddhism, evident in the prayer flags, stupas, and temples that dot the land.

Top Highlights

🗼
Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest stupas in the world, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the spiritual heart of Tibetan Buddhism in Nepal.

🌿
Chitwan National Park

Home to one-horned rhinos, Bengal tigers, and wild elephants. One of Asia's finest wildlife reserves in the lush Terai lowlands.

🏔️
Annapurna Circuit

A legendary trekking route circling the Annapurna massif — through rice terraces, alpine meadows, and high mountain passes.

🛕
Pashupatinath Temple

Nepal's most sacred Hindu temple on the banks of the Bagmati River. A place of pilgrimage, ritual, and spiritual significance.

🏘️
Bhaktapur Durbar Square

A beautifully preserved medieval city with extraordinary Newari architecture, pottery squares, and a timeless old-world atmosphere.

🌅
Pokhara & Phewa Lake

A serene lakeside city with stunning views of the Annapurna range, the gateway to adventure and a place of quiet beauty.

Culture & Festivals

Rich in tradition

Nepal's calendar overflows with festivals that bring colour, music, and devotion to every corner of the country.

  • DashainThe biggest Hindu festival — 15 days of family, feasting, and the blessings of the goddess Durga.
  • Tihar (Diwali)The "festival of lights" — five days of lamps, music, and honoring deities, crows, dogs, and siblings.
  • HoliA joyful explosion of colour marking the arrival of spring, celebrated across Nepal and India.
  • Buddha JayantiCelebrating the birth of the Buddha with candlelit processions and prayers at Lumbini and Boudhanath.

Plan Your Visit

☀️
Best Time to Visit

October–November for trekking (post-monsoon, clear skies). March–May for spring blooms and rhododendrons.

✈️
Getting There

Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu is the main entry point, with connections from major Asian hubs.

🪙
Currency & Budget

Nepali Rupee (NPR). Nepal is very affordable — budget travellers can live well on $30–50 USD per day.

🎫
Visas

Most nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival at Tribhuvan Airport. Trekking permits are required for certain routes.
